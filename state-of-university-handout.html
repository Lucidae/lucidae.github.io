UHDS Handout for the State of the University Address

University Housing & Dining Services wanted to educate attendees of the 2018 State of the University Address on what they do, and how they do it well. Part of that effort was a double-sided handout with information and images about UHDS, which is what I designed. Check out the process below!

For this project I decided to try and focus on one of Oregon State University's personality traits as shown in the university brand guidelines. I did this in an effort to communicate a stronger tone than usual. The trait I chose for this piece was confident.

Process

Step 1: Analyze
This project was unusual, because it was suddenly assigned with a quick turnaround. An email was sent to me explaining the situation with a large amount of raw text, which can be seen below (with my annotation on top of course). Due to the situation I was given free reign on how to disseminate the text and choice of sub headers.

Step 2: Sketches
Once I had settled on how I wanted to organize the text given to me, I began to sketch potential layouts.

Step 3: Digital Development
I started off working with my favorite layout sketch in InDesign. As I worked, I would occasionally do a test print to get a better grasp on how the design was developing. It would help me make stronger decisions on what to adjust.

Step 4: Review
Once I felt like the work was ready for review by my managers, I printed the handout and waited for them to evaluate it.

After making the requested changes, the handout was sent to the printer and distributed at the State of the University Address.
